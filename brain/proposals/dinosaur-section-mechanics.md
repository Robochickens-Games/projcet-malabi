# Dinosaur section mechanics

**Context:** Science Museum Mystery — dinosaur wing. Kid enters after closing time.
T-Rex skeleton is missing bones, footprints glow on the floor, fossil drawers are
open, the "Dino Timeline Machine" is malfunctioning. Goal: **repair the exhibit by
solving paleontology mysteries.**

Section working title: **The Dinosaur Hall: The Case of the Mixed-Up Fossils**

Related: [[game-concepts]], [[art-direction]], [[gameplay-principles]],
[[0003-first-product-direction-science-games-for-kids]], [[north-star]].

---

## Recommended core trio

Use 3 mechanics and reuse them in variations across the section:

### 1. Time Lens (main mechanic)
Player drags a magical/scientific museum tool over a fossil → dead exhibit becomes
a short animated "ghost scene" from the past.

- Drag over teeth → see the dinosaur eating
- Drag over footprint → see walking style / gait
- Drag over tail bones → see how it balanced
- Drag over nest → see parenting behaviour
- Drag over cracked egg → baby hatches, shadow passes overhead (player must find what scared it)

**Reference:** *Outer Wilds* — curiosity-driven discovery through environmental clues.
**Why it's the signature mechanic:** every exhibit in every wing could have a Time Lens
moment. Scales across the whole game.

### 2. Fossil Detective Board (puzzle mechanic)
Player collects evidence by tapping around the room (tooth shape, claw marks, nest
eggs, tail bones, giant footprint, broken plant fossils), then fills a simple
museum card — no text required:

```
This dinosaur was: [Plant eater] [Meat eater]
It walked on:     [Two legs]    [Four legs]
Its clue is:      [Sharp teeth] [Flat teeth] [Big claws] [Long neck]
```

Sharp teeth + claw marks + small bones nearby = predator. Deduction, not reading.

**Reference:** *Return of the Obra Dinn* (frozen-scene evidence reconstruction),
*The Case of the Golden Idol* (clue collection + deduction blanks).

### 3. Living Diorama (toy mechanic)
A frozen prehistoric diorama (volcano, jungle, lake, dinosaurs, meteor sky).
Player taps switches to change the environment:

- Rain / dry · Day / night · Plants grow/disappear
- Volcano calm/erupting · Lake full/empty

Dinosaurs react: herbivores move toward plants; predators follow; small dinosaurs
hide at night; flyers land near water. Puzzle = create the right conditions to
reveal a clue (e.g. make rain → plants grow → herbivores move → predator follows →
ground shakes → buried bone revealed).

**Reference:** *Outer Wilds* environmental cause-and-effect.
**Why it's strong:** feels like a science toy, not an exercise.

## Full loop

> Explore room → find strange fossil → use **Time Lens** → collect clue →
> test in **Diorama / lab** → solve exhibit mystery with **Detective Board** →
> unlock next museum section.

## All 15 mechanics (full list)

### 4. Rebuild the Skeleton, But It Must Balance
Jigsaw-plus-physics: drag bones onto a museum mount. If the tail is too short,
the T-Rex tips forward; mismatched legs = skeleton wobbles. Teaches balance,
anatomy, tail as counterweight. Wrong answer: skeleton collapses with a xylophone sound.
**Reference:** *The Room* — tactile physical puzzles.

### 5. Dino Footprint Path Puzzle
Museum floor has glowing fossil footprints. Player rotates tiles to connect a
path. Each dinosaur walks differently (T-Rex: two big 3-toed steps; Velociraptor:
small quick steps; Sauropod: huge slow steps) — gait IS the scientific clue.
**Reference:** *Monument Valley* — clean mobile-first spatial path puzzles.

### 6. Tooth Lab: Match Food to Dinosaur ("Chomp-O-Meter")
Three skulls: knife-like, flat grinding, peg-like stripping teeth. Player drags
food (ferns, meat, fish, pinecones) to a jaw machine. Jaw chomps. Match = museum
machine lights up. Simple, funny, visual, educational.
**Reference:** *The Room* tactile inspection + classic point-and-click object logic.

### 7. Dig Site Layer Puzzle
Excavation table: brush away sand in layers (modern objects → mammoth/ice age →
dinosaur → ammonites). Tools: brush (sand), pick (rock), magnifier (tiny fossils),
glue (cracked bones). Twist: some fossils don't belong in the dino exhibit — kid
must notice the toy car, mammoth tusk, and dinosaur bone are from different eras.
Teaches geological time without a lecture.
See also: [[puzzle-brush-away-the-dust]] (Dor's earlier proposal for this beat).

### 8. Dino Sound Echo Puzzle
Broken exhibit speaker plays dinosaur "calls" that echo strangely. Player taps
fossils/statues to reproduce the pattern. Sound = key to open exhibit cases.
Honest science note: "We don't know exactly what dinosaurs sounded like — scientists
make guesses from skulls, birds, and reptiles."

### 9. Gorogoa-Style Exhibit Panels
Museum wall: four illustrated panels (fossil bone / ancient jungle / volcano /
dinosaur skeleton). Player slides panels so visual elements connect — a river in
one lines up with a footprint in another; rib bone shape becomes an archway.
**Reference:** *Gorogoa* — illustrated panel-based visual puzzles.
**Why it's premium:** very store-screenshot-friendly and distinctive.

### 10. Dino Classification Machine
Machine asks player to classify dinosaurs by visual traits. Player drags cards
(horns, plates, long neck, sharp teeth, beak, club tail, feathers, claws) onto a
dinosaur silhouette. Correct combo unlocks the identity:
- Horns + frill + four legs = Triceratops
- Plates + spiked tail = Stegosaurus
- Long neck + four legs + plant eater = Brachiosaurus

**Reference:** *Scribblenauts* — creative object matching.

### 11. Museum Security Camera Time Scrub
Kid finds a museum camera screen showing a cute animated timeline. Player scrubs
back and forth (8:00 class enters; 8:07 strange shadow moves; 8:09 fossil drawer
opens…), pauses at the right moment, taps the clue. Reveals the "monster" is a
museum robot, escaped bird, or malfunctioning exhibit. Good for pacing the mystery.

### 12. Dino Bone X-Ray Scanner
Player places fossils into a scanner. Scanner reveals hidden things: crack lines,
fossil inside rock, fake plaster bone, tiny tooth marks, growth rings, hidden
museum symbol. Modes: X-ray / UV light / Magnify / Heat map / "Time dust."
**Reference:** *The Room* — tactile object inspection.

### 13. Predator/Prey Shadow Theater
Player rotates skeleton pieces, plants, rocks to cast the correct prehistoric
scene on a wall. "The hunter was smaller than the giant, but faster" → arrange
small raptor shadow + big herbivore shadow + tall grass. When shadows align, the
wall animates like a cave painting.
**Reference:** *Gorogoa*, *Monument Valley* — elegant visual storytelling.
Store-screenshot friendly.

### 14. Dino Egg Incubation Puzzle
Player finds fossil eggs; a museum incubator needs calibration. Controls:
temperature, humidity, nest material, light. Too hot = egg wiggles nervously;
correct range = glowing fossil pattern appears. Reveals a species symbol, not a
real hatching. Teaches environmental conditions and careful experimentation.

### 15. "Wrong Era" Sorting Game
Mixed-up objects (dinosaur bone, mammoth tusk, human spear, trilobite, meteorite,
shark tooth, gift shop toy). Player sorts them onto a giant timeline floor:
Before dinosaurs / Age of dinosaurs / After dinosaurs / Modern.
Fast, satisfying, replayable. Corrects the common misconception that humans and
non-bird dinosaurs coexisted.

## Example first quest

**"Who Broke the T-Rex Exhibit?"**

1. Kid enters dark dinosaur hall
2. T-Rex skeleton: missing tooth, tail bone, label
3. Footprints glow on floor
4. Time Lens on footprint → ghost scene: something small runs under T-Rex
5. Follow footprint path → fossil drawer opened by mistake
6. X-Ray Scanner → missing tooth inside a rock
7. Skeleton Balance puzzle → place missing tail bone
8. Detective Board → conclusion: "The cleaning robot bumped the fossil drawer"
9. T-Rex lights up, roars gently, awards museum badge

**Reward:** Paleontologist Badge
**Unlock:** Space Wing or Inventions Hall
