# ASO-Driven Theme Research — Kids Science Quest Game

_Full research run by Gidi via ChatGPT deep research, 2026-06-10._
_Informs ADR 0003 (first product direction)._

---

## Executive recommendation

**Do not launch with a single scientist name.** Build a broad, kid-friendly
science-adventure umbrella that targets high-demand topics (dinosaurs, space,
inventions), with scientists as chapter anchors — not the top-line brand.

**Highest-confidence recommendation:**
> Pick a **"Time-Travel Science Quest" or "Science Museum Mystery"** franchise theme,
> but **lead the first launch with dinosaurs** — the strongest demand signal in the set.
> Structure later chapters around space, then Leonardo's workshop.

---

## Theme ranking by ASO demand

| Theme | Exposure | Best use |
|---|---|---|
| **Dinosaur Paleontology Mystery** | **Very high** — "dinosaur games for kids" proxy 42–55; 5M+ Play downloads on comparable title; ~60k iOS monthly downloads | **Best single-world launch theme** |
| **Time-Travel Science Museum Mystery** | **High** — My Town: Museum 10M+ Play downloads, 38.4K reviews | **Best franchise umbrella** |
| **Space Observatory Adventure** | **High** — Kids Astronomy by Star Walk 2 = 1M+ downloads | Best second chapter (ages 6–10) |
| **Leonardo da Vinci Flying Workshop** | **Medium** — House of Da Vinci 500K+ Play, excellent mechanic fit, weak kid keyword reach | Best prestige / season-two chapter |
| **Science Lab Quest** | **Medium** — "science games for kids" proxy 10; 247 related apps; crowded | Best descriptor, not the hook |
| **Faraday Magnet Mystery** | **Low–medium** — no strong kid game demand signal found | Best later chapter only |

**The pattern:** dinosaurs win on demand; museum/time-travel wins on flexibility;
space wins as durable second pillar; Leonardo wins on mechanic fit. Faraday is
academically interesting but the weakest launch surface.

---

## Key insight

> "The best launch structure is not 'a game about science.' It is **'a science mystery
> with dinosaurs in the front window.'** That is the most important distinction in the research."

Generic science apps can work (PBS KIDS, Tappity), but broader breakout titles pair
learning with a much clearer fantasy, character, or environment.

---

## Recommended content roadmap

1. **Launch chapter:** Dino Detective / Dino Time Lab (paleontology)
2. **First expansion:** Lost Moon Map / Space Observatory
3. **Prestige narrative chapter:** Leonardo's Flying Workshop

This follows the strongest discoverability path: broad child fascination first →
STEM wonder → sophisticated historical-inventor craftsmanship. Keeps the app from
feeling like a loose mini-game bundle — becomes a story-driven franchise.

---

## ASO and positioning strategy

- **Separate franchise brand from search-first app title.**
  - App title: *Dino Time Lab* or *Dinosaur Mystery Quest*
  - Series label (inside app + iconography): *Science Quest*
  - Subtitle: "Tap through fossils, experiments, space clues, and inventor puzzles."
- **Treat "science" as a descriptor keyword, not the sole fantasy keyword.** Title
  and first screenshots carry the curiosity hook (dinosaur / space / mystery museum);
  subtitle and captions carry the educational promise.
- Generic educational terms ("science for kids" proxy 24–26, 190–250 related apps)
  are necessary but crowded. Concrete fascination terms get the family to tap first.

---

## Gameplay loop — the scientific quest feel

Make the scientific method **visible in the UI**, not hidden in dialogue:
- Action verbs: **Observe, Ask, Predict, Test, Record, Explain**
- Worlds feel physically embedded in science: journals, blueprints, specimen trays,
  experiment benches as puzzle stations
- Leonardo's world = sketchbook workshop; dinosaur world = dig site + fossil-prep lab;
  space world = child-friendly observatory crossed with a star atlas

Supported by education research: narrative formats deepen conceptual understanding
and make scientific ideas more accessible.

---

## Compliance — build from day one

- **Apple Kids Category** rules: parental gating, no external links/purchases
- **Google Play Families Policy** compliance
- **Teacher Approved** eligibility

Not just policy boxes — these are the store trust layer for parents. Top kid-science
titles (PBS KIDS Games, Kids Astronomy, My Town: Museum) all carry these signals.

---

## Limitations / open questions

- Estimates are directional (Google Play install buckets + ASOTools proxies), not
  exact store-volume forecasts.
- Research focused on English-language / US-global signals. If launching in Latin
  America, MENA, or non-English locales, validate localized keyword clusters separately.
- Some comparison titles are older or bundled — may distort pure theme-to-theme comparison.
