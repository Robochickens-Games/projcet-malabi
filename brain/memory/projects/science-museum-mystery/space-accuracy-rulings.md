# Space Wing — settled accuracy rulings

The space sibling of [[dino-accuracy-rulings]], produced by the [[astronomer]] skill
before the wing was built. These are the **facts the puzzles resolve to**, so they are
content spec, not trivia: in this wing the puzzle's answer *is* the fact, and a wrong
answer teaches a wrong thing no caption can undo. Append new rulings; don't re-open
settled ones.

## Solar System — the Planet Path puzzle
- **Order is Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, Neptune.** Eight
  planets. ✅
- **Pluto is a dwarf planet** (IAU, 2006) — not the ninth planet. If it appears, frame
  it as "reclassified, still fascinating," never as a joke or a demotion. ✅
- **Venus is the hottest planet (~465 °C), not Mercury** — runaway greenhouse, not
  proximity. The spec's clue ("the hottest planet isn't closest to the Sun") is ✅ and
  is the best aha in the room; keep the *reasoning* visible in the catalog, not just
  the answer.
- **"The giant with rings is sixth" is ✅ only because Saturn's rings are the visible
  ones.** Jupiter, Uranus and Neptune have rings too. ⚠️ Art constraint: do **not**
  draw visible rings on the other giants, or the clue becomes ambiguous and unfair.
- **"The red planet is fourth"** ✅ — Mars is red from **iron oxide (rust)** in its dust.
- **Scale is always a lie.** Any orrery compresses distance and inflates planet size.
  ⚠️ OK — say it once in the catalog rather than implying the spacing is real.

## Mars — Rover Repair
- **Rock ID by trait: reddish/rusty = iron-rich.** ✅ Correct, and the right thing for
  the scan puzzle to hinge on — it's the same "deduce from traits" rule as
  [[clue-design-deduction-not-naming]].
- **Solar panels really do get dusted on Mars** ✅ (dust storms ended Opportunity's
  mission). But **real rovers carry no brush** — Spirit and Opportunity had no way to
  clean their panels. ⚠️ The Solar Brush is a **game action**: acceptable as long as the
  catalog never claims rovers carry one. Frame it as museum-diorama maintenance, not
  Mars fact.
- **Rovers are slow** — centimetres per second, driven with long command delays. ✅ That
  is *why* route planning is a real job, which makes the Rover Route mini-game
  accidentally authentic. Lean into it in the catalog copy.

## Moon Missions — the landing-sequence puzzle
Verified against the NASA Apollo 11 mission overview and the Air & Space timeline.
- **Apollo 11 launched 16 July 1969; landed 20 July 1969.** ✅
- **Armstrong and Aldrin landed in the LM *Eagle*; Michael Collins stayed in lunar
  orbit in the CSM *Columbia*.** ❌ to guard against: do **not** show all three on the
  surface. Collins never walked on the Moon.
- **Canonical order for the cards** (simplify the count, never the order): launch →
  Earth orbit → trans-lunar injection → lunar orbit → LM undocks → powered descent →
  landing → EVA/first step → ascent → rendezvous & docking → trans-Earth injection →
  re-entry → splashdown. ✅
- **Twelve people have walked on the Moon** (Apollo 11–17, minus 13). ✅ All were
  American men — say "astronauts," and let the game's own child explorer carry the
  identification, so the room doesn't read as a monument to one country's 1960s.

## Space Station — Fix the Airlock
- **The ISS orbits ~400 km up, circles Earth roughly every 90 minutes, and has been
  continuously crewed since November 2000.** ✅ Not "parked in space."
- ❌ **The spec's "oxygen hose" needs reframing.** A spacewalking astronaut's oxygen
  comes from the suit's own backpack (PLSS), not a hose to the station. Astronauts are
  **tethered** and wear a **SAFER** jetpack as backup. **Ruling: keep the item, rename
  the fact** — it is an *airlock umbilical / safety tether*, not the astronaut's air
  supply. The puzzle is unchanged; the catalog line changes.
- **Solar panels must face the Sun to make power** ✅ — the Rotate Key puzzle is sound.
- **Weightlessness is free-fall, not "no gravity."** Orbit = falling around Earth and
  missing. ⚠️ Worth one honest catalog line.

## James Webb — Align the Golden Mirrors
Verified against NASA's Webb mirrors page and the NSSDCA record.
- **18 hexagonal segments, ~1.5 m point to point, beryllium substrate with a protected
  gold coating.** ✅ Gold is for **infrared** reflectivity — that's why it's gold.
- **JWST sits at the Sun–Earth L2 point, ~1.5 million km from Earth** ✅ — it does *not*
  orbit Earth like Hubble. Launched December 2021.
- **Aligning the segments and focusing is a real commissioning step** ✅ — the mirror
  puzzle is well-founded, not invented.
- **JWST sees infrared**, so its images are false-colour translations of light our eyes
  can't see. ⚠️ Beautiful and true; worth one honest catalog line rather than implying
  that's how it "looks."

## Whole-wing framing
- **No sound in space**; no flames in vacuum; stars don't twinkle above the atmosphere.
- **Real subjects only** — real spacecraft, real missions, no invented probes, per
  [[scientific-realism-rule]] and [[art-scientific-realism]].
- **Simplify, don't falsify** (ages 5–10). Space's real numbers are already astonishing;
  exaggeration is unnecessary.
