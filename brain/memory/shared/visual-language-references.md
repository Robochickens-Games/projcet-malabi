# Visual-language references — Science Museum Mystery

Companion to [[design-inspirations]] (which is a *mechanics* reference) and
[[art-direction]] (our captured style). This list is specifically about **how the
game should look and feel to the eye and the finger** — hand-illustrated, warm,
premium, tap-everything, tactile reveals. Curated for the museum-mystery shape,
ages 5–10, Apple-feature bar, no-ads premium tone.

Each entry says **what to steal**, not "copy this."

## A. Illustrated point-and-click heritage (our DNA, modernized)
- **Amanita Design — Botanicula / Samorost / Machinarium** — the closest living
  descendants of Monkey Island's *illustrated* adventure. Hand-painted, every object
  is pokeable and reacts with sound. **Botanicula** is warm, wordless, kid-safe —
  the single best tonal match. *Steal:* dense lovingly-illustrated scenes where
  curiosity is rewarded by tapping, no-text comprehension, playful reactive sound.
- **Professor Layton** (series) — detective/mystery framing, illustrated, puzzle-per-
  room, accessible to kids. *Steal:* the magnifying-glass "examine the scene for
  what's off" loop, mystery wrapper that pulls you room to room.
- **The Tiny Bang Story** — hidden-object adventure, whimsical illustrated world,
  gentle. *Steal:* hidden-object puzzles that feel like exploring a painting.

## B. Premium "Apple-feature" tactile / diorama bar
- **Gorogoa** — hand-illustrated, you zoom *into* illustrations; Apple Design Award.
  *Steal:* magnifying-glass-as-mechanic, illustration that rewards looking closer.
- **Monument Valley 1/2** (ustwo) — the cleanliness + premium polish that gets
  featured. *Steal:* restraint, perfect-feeling UI, "every frame is a poster."
- **Lumino City / Lume** (State of Play) — handmade papercraft illustration, warm,
  cozy. *Steal:* tangible "made by hand" warmth, miniature-world charm.
- **Old Man's Journey** — warm storybook illustration, rolling painterly scenes.
  *Steal:* the storybook-premium palette and lighting our concept art is already near.
- **The Room** (series) — best-in-class *tactile object manipulation* and
  examine-to-reveal. Mood is darker than ours, **borrow the touch feel not the tone**.
  *Steal:* satisfying physicality of manipulating an object to reveal a secret —
  directly relevant to [[puzzle-brush-away-the-dust]].

## C. Tap-everything / hidden-object, kid-readable
- **Hidden Folks** — every element is interactive and reacts with mouth-made sounds;
  joy is in poking the world. *Steal:* the "everything responds" density that makes
  [[gameplay-principles]]' "every tap rewards" literally true. (Mono art; we'd keep
  our color.)
- **Hidden Through Time** — warm, illustrated, kid-friendly hidden-object across
  historical scenes (incl. dinosaurs!). *Steal:* readable hidden-object in a
  themed-era diorama; gentle hint system.
- **Toca Life World / Sago Mini** — the gold standard for *under-7* "tap anything,
  something delightful happens, no fail state." *Steal:* fearless exploration, soft
  guidance, reactive micro-rewards for the youngest end of 5–10.

## D. Kids-explorable & educational, premium, no-ads (closest business + look match)
- **Tinybop — Explorer's Library (Dinosaurs / The Human Body / Mammals)** — gorgeous
  illustrated, fully-explorable science models for kids; premium, ad-free. **The most
  direct reference for "actually scientific + beautiful + tap to explore."** *Steal:*
  the explorable-diagram feel, the educational layer that never lectures, the trust-
  building ad-free premium positioning we're already aiming at ([[art-direction]]).
- **Pok Pok Playroom** — Apple Design Award, Montessori, warm hand-illustrated,
  tactile, ad-free, ages 2–8. *Steal:* the tactile open-toy feel + the exact premium
  no-ads kids-brand positioning ([[north-star]]: money via premium, fun via play).
- **Alba: A Wildlife Adventure** (ustwo) — kid-friendly explore-find-collect, gentle
  detective energy (photograph/catalog wildlife), warm illustrated, no violence.
  *Steal:* the "explore → spot → collect into a catalog" loop = our master-fossil
  fragment spine ([[puzzle-brush-away-the-dust]]).

## E. Brush / paint / reveal mechanic (for the tactile puzzles)
- **Chicory: A Colorful Tale** — a *brush* is the core verb; painting the world is the
  whole game, warm and kid-friendly. *Steal:* how good a brush stroke can feel as the
  primary touch interaction — direct inspiration for brushing dust off a fossil.
- **The Room** (again) — for the layer-by-layer "clear it to reveal what's beneath."
- **Carto** — cozy, warm, illustrated exploration-puzzle for all ages. *Steal:* the
  friendly, low-stress puzzle mood.

## How to use this
- **Tonal anchors (look + feel):** Botanicula, Tinybop, Pok Pok, Old Man's Journey.
- **Premium/feature bar:** Gorogoa, Monument Valley, The Room.
- **Mechanic feel:** Hidden Folks (tap density), Chicory/The Room (tactile reveal),
  Alba (explore-collect), Layton (mystery wrapper).
- Next step when we prototype: pull 1–2 reference screenshots per anchor into a mood
  board so the team is judging against the same bar.
