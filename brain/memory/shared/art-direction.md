# Art direction — Science Museum Mystery

Two concept images from Dor (2026-06-11):
- `brain/images/Science Museum Mystery.png` — hub lobby / UI concept (portrait, all three wings visible)
- `brain/images/landscape dino.png` — environment/atmosphere concept (landscape, dinosaur hall exterior)

Informs [[game-concepts]] and [[0003-first-product-direction-science-games-for-kids]].

## Overall style
Warm illustrated museum/adventure. Not 3D rendered — hand-illustrated, detailed,
storybook-adjacent. Premium feel. Sits close to Monkey Island / classic adventure
game illustration ([[design-inspirations]]).

## Palette
- Museum areas: **warm amber/gold** — aged wood, candlelight, stone
- Space arch/world: **cool teal/dark blue** — starfield, planets
- Accents: glowing highlights (bulbs, gems, planetary rings)
- Dark navy background — makes golden elements pop

## Architecture / setting
- Art Deco museum interior — arched doorways, ornamental framing, pendant lamps
- Three visible wings: **Dinosaurs** (left), **Space** (centre arch), **Inventions** (right)
- Space wing is a portal/arch that opens onto a starfield — sense of depth and wonder
- Museum floor: classic blue/cream tile
- Each wing reads as its own world from the lobby

## Character
- Young boy, back-facing (relatable, player-insert, not gendered front-on)
- Green jacket, khaki trousers, brown boots — adventure/explorer aesthetic
- Adventure backpack with a **space/star patch** — signals the crossover theme
- **Magnifying glass** in hand — mystery/detective framing, core mechanic signal

## Dinosaurs wing (left)
- T-Rex skeleton prominent — gold-brown fossilised bone
- Living triceratops peeking in at bottom — hint of wonder/magic
- Display placard visible — educational layer
- Dense jungle/fossil vegetation at base

## Space wing (centre arch)
- Solar system visible through the arch — planets, Saturn's rings, Earth
- Moon/asteroid on a plinth: "MOON ROCK" label — touchable exhibit
- Red rocket in the arch — classic, friendly, not sci-fi
- Convex glass dome on exhibit — interactive display signal

## Inventions wing (right)
- Steampunk/Victorian: gears, pipes, clocks, gramophone, biplane model
- Blueprint/sketch on a lectern — Leonardo-adjacent without using his name
- Warm brass/copper tones — distinct from the other two wings

## Bottom feature strip (store screenshots)
Three icon + label callout cards:
1. 🔍 **Search for hidden clues** — finger tapping a fossil in foliage
2. 🧠 **Learn by exploring** — child tapping a tablet showing Saturn
3. 🎁 **A museum full of surprises** — glowing treasure chest with crystals

## Store trust badges (bottom bar)
- ✅ **Kid-Safe. No ads. Ever.** — strong parent trust signal; no ad model confirmed
- 🎓 **Ages 5–10. Made for curious minds** — slightly broader/younger than research target of 6–9
- ⬇️ **Play Offline. Anytime, anywhere** — important for school/travel contexts

## Tone
Not scary, not dumbed-down. Curious, warm, slightly mysterious. "You're the detective
in a museum that's more than it looks." Every corner has something to tap.

## Landscape / environment reference (`brain/images/landscape dino.png`)
Second concept from Dor — establishes the outdoor/environmental mood for the
dinosaur section and possibly the museum exterior:
- Scale and grandeur: giant prehistoric creature dwarfs the landscape
- Painterly, editorial illustration style — not pixel, not vector-flat; textured brush strokes
- Rich, slightly muted naturalistic palette: dusty terracotta/ochre earth, deep olive greens, overcast sky
- Cinematic wide composition — horizon, open sky, sense of time and depth
- Subject off-centre with strong negative space — emphasises wonder and isolation
- Tone: awe without fear; the scale is magical, not threatening
- **Application:** use this palette + scale reference for dino-wing environment art; the lobby
  concept handles UI/navigation while this drives the in-wing scene composition.
