# ASO research findings — kids science quest (2026-06-10)

Research run by Gidi via ChatGPT deep research. Full report in
`brain/research/aso-keyword-research-2026-06-10.md`. Informs [[0003-first-product-direction-science-games-for-kids]].

## The verdict: lead with dinosaurs

**Dinosaurs are the strongest demand + science overlap in the set.** Launch first,
then expand to space, then Leonardo.

Theme ranking:
1. **Dinosaurs** — Very high demand (proxy 42–55). Best first launch.
2. **Time-travel / museum umbrella** — High. Best franchise wrapper.
3. **Space** — High. Best second chapter.
4. **Leonardo da Vinci** — Medium demand, excellent mechanic fit. Best prestige chapter.
5. **Science Lab** / **Faraday** — Medium / Low. Too crowded or too niche to lead.

## Key positioning insight

> "Not a game about science — a science mystery with dinosaurs in the front window."

App title = *Dino Time Lab* or *Dinosaur Mystery Quest* (demand hook).
Series brand = *Science Quest* (franchise identity).

## Content roadmap
1. Launch: Dino Detective / Dino Time Lab
2. Expansion: Lost Moon Map / Space Observatory
3. Prestige: Leonardo's Flying Workshop

## Gameplay loop
Scientific method visible in UI: **Observe → Ask → Predict → Test → Record → Explain**.
Worlds feel physically embedded: dig sites, fossil-prep labs, observatory dials, sketchbook workshops.

## Watch out for
- Build for Apple Kids Category + Google Families compliance from day one — it's a
  store trust signal parents actively look for.
- "Science" is a descriptor keyword, not the fantasy hook — concrete themes (dinosaur,
  space, mystery) get the first tap.
